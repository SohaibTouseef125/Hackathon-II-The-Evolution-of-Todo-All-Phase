## Background Questions (5-10 minutes)

1. Can you tell me about your role and what you do?
2. How long have you been [using this type of product/service]?
3. What does a typical day look like for you in relation to [product/service area]?
4. What tools do you currently use for [relevant tasks]?

## Main Interview Questions

### Current Workflow
1. Walk me through how you currently [perform the main task].
   - What tools do you use?
   - What steps do you follow?
   - How long does this typically take?

2. What challenges do you face when trying to [achieve the goal]?
   - What frustrates you most?
   - What takes the longest?
   - What do you wish was easier?

### Pain Points and Needs
3. What's the most difficult part about [current process]?
4. What would make your job easier?
5. What do you like about your current solution?
6. What would cause you to switch to a different solution?

### Future Vision
7. If you could change one thing about [current process], what would it be?
8. What would your ideal solution look like?
9. How do you think [relevant area] will change in the future?

1. Is there anything important about [topic] that we haven't discussed?
2. What questions do you have for me?
3. Would you be interested in participating in future research?

## Analysis Framework

### Themes to Look For
- **Efficiency**: Time, steps, complexity
- **Effectiveness**: Success rate, accuracy, completeness
- **Satisfaction**: Ease, frustration, confidence
- **Workarounds**: How users adapt to limitations

### Success Indicators
- Clear understanding of user needs
- Identification of specific pain points
- Insights that inform design decisions
- Validation of assumptions
